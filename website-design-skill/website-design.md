# Website Design

From Ruskin Consulting Training Module 37.

A well-designed website builds trust, generates leads, and supports every paid and organic marketing effort. A poorly designed website wastes every dollar spent driving traffic to it. Evaluating and improving client websites is part of the job.

**For standalone campaign pages used with paid traffic, also reference the landing-pages skill. The two skills work together.**

---

## The 20 Components of a High-Converting Website

Every client website should be evaluated against all 20 of these. Flag anything missing or poorly executed and provide a specific recommendation.

---

### 1. Logo
A 250x100 pixel image representing the brand. Should sit in the top-left corner of every page. Check that it is present, properly sized, and links back to the homepage.

---

### 2. Favicon
A 16x16 pixel image that appears in browser tabs, bookmarks, and search history. Small detail but it signals professionalism and helps users find the tab when they have multiple open.

---

### 3. Color Scheme
Color accounts for up to 60% of a visitor's acceptance or rejection of a product or service. A bad color combination has the same negative impact as poor copy or slow load times.

**How to evaluate:**
- Does the color scheme match the brand identity?
- Are colors used consistently across all pages?
- Do the colors evoke the right emotional response for the audience?

**Color psychology reference:**

| Color | Common Emotional Association |
|---|---|
| Blue | Trust, calm, professionalism |
| Red | Urgency, energy, passion |
| Green | Health, growth, money |
| Orange | Enthusiasm, creativity, affordability |
| Black | Luxury, sophistication, authority |
| Yellow | Optimism, warmth, caution |
| Purple | Wisdom, creativity, premium |

When choosing a palette, start with the dominant brand color and build around it using complementary or analogous colors from the color wheel. Darker colors carry more visual weight and are seen first.

---

### 4. Title Tag
The clickable headline shown on search engine results pages. Should be an accurate, concise description of the page content. Critical for both SEO and click-through rate in organic search. Check that every page has a unique, keyword-relevant title tag.

---

### 5. Headings
HTML heading hierarchy (H1 through H6) in descending order of importance. The H1 should be at the top of every page and contain the primary keyword for that page. Use H2 for section headers within the page. Never skip heading levels.

---

### 6. Header
The section at the top of every page. Should contain the logo, navigation bar, and primary CTA. A good header establishes the brand, makes the site easy to navigate, and presents the conversion action immediately.

---

### 7. Navigation Bar
Links placed horizontally within the header. Visitors should be able to find what they need without help. Every main service, product category, and contact option should be reachable from the nav bar. If a visitor cannot find something in the nav, they will leave.

---

### 8. Call-to-Action (CTA)
A button in the header and at key points throughout the page that drives the primary conversion. Must provoke an immediate response.

**Effective CTAs:**
- "Call Now"
- "Request an Appointment"
- "Get a Free Quote"
- "Sign Up Today"
- "Download Now"

The CTA must use action words, stand out visually with a contrasting color, and be above the fold. See the landing-pages skill for deeper CTA guidance specific to campaign pages.

---

### 9. Banner
The section below the header on the homepage. Contains a strong image and the business tagline. The image should show a person benefiting from the product or service. Avoid generic stock photos wherever possible.

---

### 10. Body
The main content area that changes from page to page. Should be specific to each service or product. This is where most SEO value is created. Check that body content is unique per page, not duplicated across the site.

---

### 11. Contact Forms
Forms that capture Name, Phone Number, Email, and Message at minimum. B2B businesses should also include Job Title and Company Name. Every form must work correctly and submit somewhere that leads are tracked. Test every form during the initial account check-in (see account-checkin-ash skill).

---

### 12. Testimonials
Customer reviews and quotes that establish credibility and increase conversion rate. 92% of people read testimonials before making a purchase. Every service business website should have testimonials visible on the homepage and relevant service pages.

**Questions to help a client collect testimonials:**
- Why did you decide to work with us?
- What benefits did you experience working with us?
- Who else do you think would benefit from our services?

---

### 13. Case Studies
More detailed than testimonials, case studies tell the story of how the business improved a client's results over time. Most relevant for B2B companies. Case studies demonstrate expertise, decision-making, and results in a way a testimonial cannot.

---

### 14. Contact Page
A dedicated page with all contact information. Must include at minimum: a contact form, phone number, and email address. For local businesses, include a Google Maps embed and hours of operation. If a visitor cannot find how to contact the business, they will go to a competitor.

---

### 15. Footer
The strip across the bottom of every page. Contains copyright notices, quick links, and secondary contact info. Social media links typically live here.

---

### 16. Social Media Links
Links to active social media profiles, typically in the footer. Only link to profiles that are active and maintained. A dead social media profile linked from the site hurts credibility more than having no link at all.

---

### 17. Sitemap
An XML file that tells search engines about every page on the site and how they relate. Required for any site over 500 pages. Even for smaller sites, a sitemap improves crawling speed. Check that the sitemap is submitted in Google Search Console.

---

### 18. Terms and Conditions
A legally binding agreement between the business and its users. Required for any site that collects user data or facilitates transactions. Can be generated at TermsFeed if the client does not have one.

---

### 19. Privacy Policy
Details how the business collects and uses visitor data. Required by law in many countries if personal information is collected. Also required by Google Analytics and Google Ads. Flag this immediately if it is missing.

---

### 20. FAQ Page
Answers to the most common questions about the business, products, or services. Reduces inbound support requests, improves SEO by targeting question-based searches, and helps users self-qualify. Good FAQ content often maps directly to high-intent search terms worth targeting in paid campaigns.

## Mobile-First and Core Web Vitals

Google uses mobile-first indexing, meaning the mobile version of a site is what Google primarily evaluates for rankings. A site that looks fine on desktop but breaks or loads slowly on mobile will underperform in search and lose paid traffic.

Every client site must be evaluated on mobile, not just desktop.

**Core Web Vitals** are Google's three key performance metrics. They are direct ranking signals and affect both organic rankings and landing page experience scores in Google Ads:

| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|---|---|---|
| LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) | How fast the main content loads | Under 2.5 seconds |
| CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) | How much the layout shifts while loading | Under 0.1 |
| INP (Interaction to Next Paint) | How fast the page responds to clicks and taps | Under 200 milliseconds |

Use Google PageSpeed Insights to check all three. A score of 90+ on desktop is achievable for most sites. On mobile, 70+ is a solid floor with 90+ being excellent. Poor Core Web Vitals hurt both organic rankings and Google Ads Quality Score.

**Schema Markup (Structured Data)**

For local service businesses, adding LocalBusiness schema to the homepage and contact page tells Google the business name, address, phone number, service area, and hours in a machine-readable format. This improves visibility in local search results, Knowledge Panels, and can feed directly into Google Ads location assets. Flag missing schema as a recommendation during any website audit.

## Website Audit Checklist

Use this during the initial account check-in (ASH) or any time a website review is needed.

**Technical:**
- [ ] Site is on HTTPS — check that the URL shows a padlock and all pages redirect from HTTP to HTTPS. Flag immediately if missing.
- [ ] Google PageSpeed Insights checked on both mobile and desktop (90+ desktop, 70+ mobile floor)
- [ ] Core Web Vitals reviewed: LCP under 2.5s, CLS under 0.1, INP under 200ms
- [ ] Site is mobile responsive and tested on a real mobile device or emulator
- [ ] All pages load without errors — no broken pages or 404s
- [ ] Contact forms submit correctly and leads are tracked
- [ ] Phone numbers and emails are clickable on mobile
- [ ] Sitemap exists and is submitted to Search Console
- [ ] Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions pages exist
- [ ] No HTTP links on an HTTPS site (mixed content errors)

**Content and Design:**
- [ ] All 20 components present or accounted for
- [ ] Color scheme is consistent and appropriate for the brand
- [ ] Each service or product has its own dedicated page
- [ ] H1 tags present and keyword-relevant on every page
- [ ] Title tags unique and descriptive on every page
- [ ] Testimonials visible on the homepage and service pages
- [ ] CTA clear, above the fold, and on every key page

**SEO and Structured Data:**
- [ ] LocalBusiness schema present (for local service businesses)
- [ ] Google Search Console connected and no crawl errors flagged
- [ ] No duplicate content across pages

**Ad Campaign Readiness:**
- [ ] A specific landing page exists for every planned ad group
- [ ] Each landing page matches the search term theme of its ad group
- [ ] Conversion tracking fires correctly on thank-you pages and form submissions
- [ ] No campaigns pointing only to the homepage

## When to Flag a Website Issue to the Client

You are responsible for the success of the client's landing pages unless the client refuses your recommendation. Flag problems immediately and be specific about the fix.

| Problem | What to Tell the Client |
|---|---|
| No HTTPS | Flag as urgent. Browsers warn users on HTTP sites and Google de-prioritizes them in rankings. Most hosts enable SSL for free. |
| Slow page speed | Share the PageSpeed Insights report and list the top 3 fixes |
| Poor Core Web Vitals | Share the specific metric failing, the target, and the recommended fix |
| Missing landing page | Specify exactly which page needs to be created and what it should cover |
| Broken forms | Report immediately and request a fix before the campaign continues spending |
| Weak or missing CTA | Show examples of better CTAs and specify placement |
| Poor design | Show 2-3 better examples and explain the impact on conversions |
| No Privacy Policy | Flag as urgent, it is a legal requirement and blocks Google Analytics |
| Not mobile responsive | Show screenshots of the breakage and request a fix before launching paid campaigns |

Document every recommendation and the client's response. If performance suffers because the client refused a documented recommendation, that is on record.
