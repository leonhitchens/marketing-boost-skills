# Landing Pages

From Ruskin Consulting Training Module 38.

A landing page is a standalone page separate from the main website. It has one goal, one purpose, and one call-to-action. Landing pages are used to capture leads or drive sales. You will almost always get better results sending paid traffic to a dedicated landing page than to the homepage or any general website page.

**For the full website beyond the landing page, also reference the website-design skill. The two skills work together.**

---

## 2 Types of Landing Pages

### Lead Capture Page
Offers something valuable for free (webinar, checklist, ebook, free consultation) in exchange for contact information. Used when the sales cycle is long and the customer needs to be nurtured before they buy.

Use a lead capture page when:
- The product or service is complex or expensive
- The customer typically researches for weeks or months before purchasing
- The goal is to get the customer into a follow-up sequence (email, phone, retargeting)

### Sales Page
Created purely to generate a sale. Longer than lead capture pages because more copy is needed to justify the purchase.

Use a sales page when:
- The product is expensive or complicated and needs explanation
- The product is relatively new and prospects need to be educated
- The goal is a direct purchase or signed contract

## The 15-Second Rule

Users spend less than 15 seconds on a page before deciding whether to stay or leave. The page must immediately confirm the user is in the right place. If the headline and image do not answer the user's question within 15 seconds, they are gone.

79% of users scan pages rather than read them. Design and write copy for scanners, not readers.

**The three questions every visitor asks on arrival:**
1. What is this site about?
2. What can I do here, and is it what I am looking for?
3. How is it useful to me?

The headline, banner image, and CTA must answer all three within the first screenful.

---

## 5 Tips for Readability and Conversion

1. **Use headings.** Makes users stop and actually read the content. Each section needs a clear H2 or H3.
2. **Use white space.** Breaks up the page and increases readability. Crowded pages feel overwhelming and people leave.
3. **Use high-quality images.** The right image communicates values and culture faster than any copy. Avoid generic stock photos.
4. **Use bulleted lists.** Draws attention to important information and makes scanning easy.
5. **Write short sentences and paragraphs.** Keeps attention. If a sentence can be cut in two, cut it.

---

## Above the Fold

Above the fold is what the visitor sees before scrolling. 80% of users decide whether to stay or leave based solely on what is above the fold.

Above the fold must always contain:
- A clear headline
- A strong banner image
- A visible CTA button

Never put the CTA only at the bottom of the page.

---

## Remove the Navigation Bar

A landing page should have no main navigation bar, or at most a stripped-down version with only the logo and a phone number. Every navigation link is an exit ramp off the page. A visitor who clicks away to read the About page is a lost conversion. The only place they should go is to fill out the form or make a call.

---

## Headlines

The headline is the most important copy element on the page. Get it wrong and nothing else matters.

**3 best practices:**
1. **Clarity.** One short sentence that tells the user exactly what the page is about.
2. **Message match.** The headline should mirror the ad or search term that brought the user there. If the ad said "Emergency Dentist in Manhattan," the headline should say the same or very close to it.
3. **Short.** 5 to 10 words. No more.

**4 headline formulas:**

| Formula | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Action Headline | States the desired result or benefit | "Get Whiter Teeth in One Visit" |
| Number Headline | Uses a number to promise a clear result | "7 Ways to Lower Your Dental Bills This Year" |
| Question Headline | Poses a question the user is already asking | "Still Dealing With Back Pain Every Day?" |
| What You Do and Who You Help | Direct statement for a specific audience | "Tax Filing Services for Freelancers and Contractors" |

---

## Banner Image

The first visual a visitor sees. It sets the emotional tone of the entire page.

**Best practices:**
- Visually represents what the page is about
- Shows a person experiencing a positive result from the product or service
- Avoids generic stock photography wherever possible
- Supports the headline, does not compete with it

---

## Call-to-Action (CTA) Button

**Guidelines for a high-converting CTA:**
- Use action words: get, book, register, claim, download, start
- Use a bright contrasting color that stands out from the rest of the page
- Place it above the fold so users see it before scrolling
- Tell users exactly what happens when they click ("Book a Free Consultation," not just "Submit")
- Repeat the CTA before the footer at the bottom of the page. Some users scroll all the way down before deciding. Give them one more chance.

## Contact Forms

Keep forms short. Every additional field reduces conversion rate. For most lead gen pages, three fields is the target: Name, Phone Number, and Email. Message or service type can be added if it helps qualify leads, but every extra field costs you conversions.

B2B pages can justify more fields (Company Name, Job Title) when the lead quality gain outweighs the conversion rate loss. Test and measure.

Never ask for information the business does not actually need to follow up.

## Social Proof

83% of people trust social proof over advertising. Every landing page should include at least one form of social proof.

**4 ways to demonstrate social proof:**

1. **As Featured In logos.** Publications, media, or websites where the business has been featured or reviewed.
2. **Client logos.** Current or past client logos. Particularly effective for B2B.
3. **Certifications and qualifications.** Industry credentials, awards, accreditations. (Google Partner badge, BBB rating, industry association membership.)
4. **Testimonials.** The most powerful form. 92% of people read testimonials before making a purchase.

**Placement matters.** Put at least one testimonial or certification logo directly next to or below the CTA button. Social proof placed at the moment of decision reduces hesitation and increases clicks. Do not bury all social proof at the bottom of the page.

**Questions to collect strong testimonials:**
- Why did you decide to work with us?
- What benefits did you experience working with us?
- Who else do you think would benefit from our services?

## 3 Ways to Boost Conversion Rate

The average landing page conversion rate across all industries is 2.35%. The top 10% convert at 11.45% or higher. These three additions consistently push pages toward the top.

### 1. Explainer Video
A short video explaining what the business does and why the visitor should take action.

**Script structure:**
1. State the problem the visitor is experiencing
2. Introduce the solution the business provides
3. Explain key benefits and how it works
4. End with a clear call-to-action

**Video tips:**
- Use a strong thumbnail
- Hook the viewer in the first 3 seconds
- Keep it under 2 minutes for most service businesses

### 2. Exit Intent Popup
A message that appears when a user moves their cursor toward closing the browser. Most commonly used to present a reason to stay or an alternative offer (free consultation, discount, downloadable resource). Captures leads that would otherwise leave with nothing.

### 3. Countdown Timer
Creates urgency and scarcity. Tells the visitor they must act within a specific window to receive the offer. Use only when the urgency is real.

## Message Match

Message match is the most overlooked factor in landing page conversion. The headline, copy, and imagery on the landing page must match the ad that brought the visitor there.

If the ad says "Affordable Dental Implants in Manhattan," the landing page headline must reflect that exact promise. A visitor who lands on a generic homepage will bounce immediately because nothing confirmed they are in the right place.

Every ad group should have a corresponding landing page that matches its primary search term. This also directly improves Quality Score and Landing Page Experience in Google Ads, which lowers CPC and improves Ad Rank. See the ad-optimization skill for how Landing Page Experience feeds into Ad Rank.

---

## Page Speed

Landing pages must load fast. Every additional second of load time costs conversions. The target is under 3 seconds. Check with Google PageSpeed Insights.

The biggest culprits that slow landing pages:
- Too many third-party scripts (chat widgets, analytics pixels, social embeds, tag managers loading unnecessary tags)
- Large uncompressed images
- Unoptimized video embeds
- Fonts loading from external servers

Strip landing pages lean. Every script that is not essential to the conversion goal should be removed or deferred. A landing page does not need a live chat widget, a Facebook pixel, three analytics tags, and two font libraries all loading at once.

## The Thank-You Page

The thank-you page is what a visitor sees after submitting a form. Most businesses treat it as an afterthought. It should not be.

**What a thank-you page must do:**
1. Confirm the submission so the visitor knows it worked ("Your request has been received.")
2. Tell the visitor exactly what happens next ("We will call you within one business day.")
3. Set expectations to reduce anxiety and no-shows

**What a thank-you page can also do:**
- Offer a next step (book a call directly, download a resource, follow on social)
- Cross-sell a related service
- Ask for a referral while intent is highest

The thank-you page is also where the conversion event fires in Google Ads and GA4. Verify it is tracking correctly on every account.

## Landing Page Audit Checklist

Use this when reviewing any landing page receiving paid traffic.

**Above the Fold:**
- [ ] Headline is clear, 5-10 words, and matches the ad or search term
- [ ] Banner image is high quality and relevant to the offer
- [ ] CTA button is visible without scrolling
- [ ] CTA uses action words and tells the user what happens when they click

**Body:**
- [ ] Page answers all 3 visitor questions (what is this, can I do it here, how does it help me)
- [ ] Content uses headings, white space, bullets, and short paragraphs
- [ ] At least one form of social proof is present
- [ ] Social proof is placed near the CTA, not only at the bottom
- [ ] Navigation bar is removed or stripped down to logo and phone number only
- [ ] CTA is repeated before the footer
- [ ] Form has 3 fields or fewer for lead gen (Name, Phone, Email) unless B2B justifies more

**Technical:**
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds on mobile (check Google PageSpeed Insights)
- [ ] Page is on HTTPS
- [ ] Page is mobile responsive
- [ ] Third-party scripts are minimized — no unnecessary chat widgets, social embeds, or extra pixels
- [ ] Form submits correctly and confirmation page fires the conversion event
- [ ] Conversion tracking active and recording in Google Ads and GA4
- [ ] Thank-you page confirms submission and sets expectations for next steps

**Message Match:**
- [ ] Headline matches the primary search term and ad copy
- [ ] Page content is specific to the campaign, not generic
- [ ] The offer on the page matches the offer in the ad
